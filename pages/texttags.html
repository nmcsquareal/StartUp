Text tags

One of the things that you will mostly insert in your websites are texts. In this section, you will learn how to add different type of text formatting to achieve your desirable result. We will start with headings, paragraph and some text-formatting elements such as bold, italicized superscript and subscript.


Headings

HTML headings are titles or subtitles that you want to display on a webpage. Presented below are different type of headings that you may use in your website.

<h1>Heading 1<h1>

<h2>Heading 2<h2>

<h3>Heading 3<h3>

<h4>Heading 4<h4>

<h5>Heading 5<h5>
<h6>Heading 6<h6>

You can use headings from <h1> (largest) to <h6> (smallest). By enclosing a text to a heading tag, you make the content a title or a subtitle.

Paragraph

The paragraph <p> tag declares a paragraph. Browsers automatically add a single blank line before and after each paragraph element.

Below is an example of an html document with a heading and paragraph tag on its body.

<!DOCTYPE html>

<html>

<head>


</head>

<body>

<h1> This is a large header </h1>

<p> This is a paragraph </p>

<p> This is another paragraph </p>

<p> Notice that another paragraph will automatically go to the next line </p>

<h2> This is a header but smaller than h1 </h2>

<p> This is the last paragraph </p>

</body>

</html>

<mark>Marked text
